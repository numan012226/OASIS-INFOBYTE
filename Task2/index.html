Biography

Ratan Naval Tata (born 28 December 1937) is an Indian industrialist, philanthropist, and a former chairman of Tata Sons. He was also the chairman of Tata Group, India's largest conglomerate, from 1990 to 2012 and again, as interim chairman, from October 2016 through February 2017, and continues to head its charitable trusts.

Early Life

Ratan Tata was born into the prominent Tata family, a prominent family of Indian industrialists and philanthropists. He is the son of Naval Tata and Sooni Tata. He studied at the Campion School, Mumbai, and later attended the Cathedral and John Connon School, also in Mumbai. He graduated from Riverdale Country School in New York City in 1955. He then completed a B.S. degree in architecture with structural engineering from Cornell University in 1959, and later the Advanced Management Program from Harvard Business School in 1975.

Career

Ratan Tata began his career in the Tata group in 1961. He initially started on the shop floor of Tata Steel, shoveling limestone and handling the blast furnace. In 1991, J.R.D. Tata stepped down as chairman of Tata Sons, naming him his successor. Under his stewardship, Tata Group went through a major transformation, transforming it into a global business conglomerate with a presence in numerous countries. Some of the major achievements during his tenure include the acquisition of Tetley, Jaguar Land Rover, and Corus, making Tata Group an influential global business entity.

Philanthropy

Ratan Tata has been involved in numerous philanthropic activities and initiatives. He is a strong advocate for education, medicine, and rural development. The Tata Education and Development Trust, a philanthropic affiliate of Tata Group, endowed a $28 million Tata Scholarship Fund that will allow Cornell University to provide financial aid to undergraduate students from India. He has also contributed to causes such as health, child development, and women's rights.

Awards and Recognition

Ratan Tata has received several awards and honors for his contributions to business and philanthropy. Some of these include the Padma Vibhushan, India's second-highest civilian award, in 2008, and the Padma Bhushan, the third-highest civilian honor in 2000. He has also been conferred with honorary degrees from several universities and institutions.

Major Achievements

  • 1998

    Launch of Tata Indica

    In 1998, Tata Motors launched the Tata Indica, India's first indigenously developed car. It became a significant milestone in the Indian automobile industry.

  • 2008

    Acquisition of Jaguar Land Rover

    In 2008, Tata Motors acquired the British luxury carmaker Jaguar Land Rover, marking a significant achievement in Tata Group's global expansion.

  • 2012

    Retirement

    Ratan Tata retired as chairman of Tata Sons in 2012, having transformed the Tata Group into a global powerhouse with a market capitalization of over $100 billion.
